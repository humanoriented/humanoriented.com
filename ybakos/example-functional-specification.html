Example Functional Specification

The Problem

The last time I was in Las Vegas I paused to wonder at how much money gets lost there by all us visitors. The hippie in me wondered what could be possible if all that money were directed toward a more serious cause than fun and gambling.

My problem is that I don't like to gamble. And I have yet to understand why lots of businesses and project teams love to gamble -- every time they try to take on a mid-to-large project without the benefit of a functional specification. C'mon no whammies!

How do you communicate that you want the button 32 pixels from the bottom and left of the control pane and what the user should experience when he clicks on it, to five or more people, without repeating yourself? How can you define a standard that the application must meet in order to be considered complete? Have you really thought out each and every portion of the application and its potential pitfalls by sketching it out?

A functional specification is not a rigid thing corporations sign off on. It's not so long that it needs a binder. A functional specification is a brief design document that communicates what the application is, how it works for the user and what it should roughly look like on the screen. It should be terse without sacrificing completeness and preferably entertaining (or at least not too mind-numbing).
