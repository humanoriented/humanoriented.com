The Problem (a rant against SEO)

Everyone wants to be on the top of google, and boy do I mean everyone. A whole new service market has emerged just because of this, called Search Engine Optimization. One task of SEO is to attain a higher "keyword density" in content. So if your content is 100 words and ten of those words are "banana," your keyword density for banana is 1:10. Keyword density is theorized to be one of the factors that most search engines use in ranking results. So if you want to be on the top of google for searches of "banana" you'd best try to at least have a high keyword density for banana. Capice?

What's SEO have to do with Python? Nothing! But one day I wanted to answer "What are the most frequently appearing words in this here piece of content?" and decided to answer that with a Python script. I'm still working on it, but here it is.
